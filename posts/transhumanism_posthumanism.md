# Discussion
To transition into computers, the problem amounts to: how to do so with limited resources?
If you can afford new autonomous tools, this is how to transplant ([*Central Nervous Systems* (**CNS**s)](https://wikipedia.org/wiki/Central_nervous_system) plus [*Peripheral Nervous Systems* (**PNS**s)](https://wikipedia.org/wiki/Peripheral_nervous_system) (composed of neural tissue) into computer systems (composed of silicone plus transistors).

Search for [architectures of human *Central Nervous Systems*](https://google.com/search?q=Architecture+of+human+central+nervous+system), produce autonomous tools which cool your tissue until almost frozen, then have your heart stop, then transplant your **CNS** into computer systems (plus some synthetic skeleton which replaces your **PNS** (composed of biological muscles / sensors) with synthetic servos / sensors)

You can rent simple medical robots in 2024:
- <https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC3016020/>
- <https://finmodelslab.com/blogs/operating-costs/medical-robotics-operating-costs>

, but most of those just have 1 or 2 arms plus can not do much more than drill a hole through your cranium to rip your cortex (plus spinal cord) out of you.
